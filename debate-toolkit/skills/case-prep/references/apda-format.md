# APDA Parliamentary Debate Format

## Overview

The American Parliamentary Debate Association (APDA) uses a British Parliamentary-inspired format with two teams of two debaters each. Government (Gov) defends the motion; Opposition (Opp) negates it.

## Speech Order and Times

| Speech              | Speaker        | Time     |
|---------------------|----------------|----------|
| Prime Minister (PM) | Gov 1st speaker | 7 min   |
| Leader of Opp (LO)  | Opp 1st speaker | 8 min   |
| Member of Gov (MG)  | Gov 2nd speaker | 8 min   |
| Member of Opp (MO)  | Opp 2nd speaker | 8 min   |
| Leader of Opp Rebuttal (LOR) | Opp 1st speaker | 4 min |
| Prime Minister Rebuttal (PMR) | Gov 1st speaker | 5 min |

## Points of Information (POIs)

- Available during constructive speeches (PM, LO, MG, MO) after the first minute and before the last minute
- Not available during rebuttals
- Speaker may accept or decline
- Stand, state POI briefly (under 15 seconds)
- Accepting 1-2 POIs per speech is considered good form

## Motion Types

- **Tight link**: Specific policy or position (e.g., "This house would ban private prisons")
- **Loose link**: Broad theme the Gov can interpret (e.g., "This house believes in second chances")
- **Truism**: A motion so obviously true it's unfair -- Opp can call a "point of order" if Gov runs a truism

## Strategic Considerations

### For Government
- Control definitions early in the PM speech
- Choose an interpretation that is debatable but favors your side
- The PM must lay out the full case; the MG extends and rebuilds
- PMR cannot introduce new arguments, only respond to Opp's case and rebuild Gov's

### For Opposition
- The LO must engage directly with the PM's case before building Opp's own arguments
- The MO should extend the Opp case and attack the MG's extensions
- LOR is purely rebuttal: no new arguments, crystallize the key voting issues

### General Tips
- Weigh arguments by magnitude, probability, and reversibility of impact
- Use specific examples over abstract philosophy when possible
- Signpost clearly: "My first contention is...", "Turning to their second argument..."
- Time management: spend roughly 60% on your own case, 40% on responding to the other side
- In rebuttals, identify the 2-3 most important clashes and explain why you win each one
